## Conflicts

- Same-person conflict: the adult and child Dario rows must remain distinct. Age 64 versus age 11 on the same date makes a same-person reading impossible.
- Duplicate-person risk: high if the child passenger is merged into `Dario Arturo Pulgar-Smith` or the adult passenger is merged into `Dario Pulgar A.` / `Dario Jose Pulgar-Arriagada` by name alone.
- Name-variant conflict: the 1953 passenger list supports only `Dario Pulgar`. It does not prove `Dario Arturo`, `Pulgar-Smith`, `Dario Jose`, `Pulgar-Arriagada`, or `Dario Pulgar A.` as controlled variants.
- Relationship-conflict evidence: the list suggests a grouped Pulgar party but does not explicitly state Dorothy's relationship to either Dario or the younger Dario's relationship to the adult Dario.
- Chronology-conflict evidence: adult age 64 in 1953 is chronologically compatible with 1928 `Dario Pulgar A.` age 39, but this is not a proof bridge. Child age 11 in 1953 is chronologically compatible with later CV schooling beginning in 1954, but this is not a proof bridge.
- Conversion-confidence conflict: the principal identity distinction is strong; the child last-address ditto and some compared Pulgar-line clusters remain held for targeted QA.

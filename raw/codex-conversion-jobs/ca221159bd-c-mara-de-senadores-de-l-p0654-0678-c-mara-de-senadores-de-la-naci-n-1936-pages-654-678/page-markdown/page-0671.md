ARGENTINE TRANSPORT
BILLS

DEBATE IN THE SENATE
FROM OUR OWN CORRESPONDENT
BUENOS AIRES, AUG. 2
THE Argentine Senate yesterday began a
debate on the two Transport Coordination
Bills.
Senator Landaburu, a supporter of the
Government, moved the appointment of a
committee of five Senators to consider them.
He said the possible renewal of the Anglo-
Argentine treaty bore no relation to transport
coordination, the latter being a free and
publicly discussed law, the former an inter-
national treaty.
Senator Palacios (Socialist) protested at what
he called the intervention of foreign Govern-
ments in Argentine internal affairs. He said
that in November, 1931, the British, French,
and Belgian Ambassadors had urged the
Government to increase tram fares, and he
quoted more recent examples of interference.
The English, he said, had tried to exercise
pressure on the authorities of the country to
obtain laws favourable to foreign capitalists,
but Argentina was a free country with demo-
cratic institutions, not a Dominion or a Crown
Colony.
The Transport Coordination Bills, which
passed the Chamber of Deputies last Septem-
ber, are designed to protect the railways and
the Buenos Aires tramways, in which much
foreign, particularly British, capital is involved,
against unfair competition by motor transport.

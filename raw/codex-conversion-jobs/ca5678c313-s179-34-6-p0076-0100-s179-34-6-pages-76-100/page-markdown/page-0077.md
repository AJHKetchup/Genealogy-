He offered to take me to the station to meet the train from
Montevideo who was d[ue] to arrive at 6 p.m.
I then went to the Hotel Scanavino and spoke to the porter who
said that he himself does not take anybody accross. After waiting with
my drink a short time, I was approached by a man who said his name was
Tanini who inquired how many people I had, I replied two women and myself.
He said that it would cost me ten Pesos a piece, and he would see that I
get over to the Argentine side at 9 p.m. the same night. I asked when is
the money to be paid and he replied "before you start". I then said what
assurance do I have that I get there and he replied "Do not worry I give
you your money back if I do not get you over". He said that he is doing
a lot of business for this hotel. He seemed to be Italian.
I then went to Mme Fifi a French women who keeps a disorderly
house on Reconquista street. She had several girls mostly natives. I spoke
to her about othervthings and casually inquired whether I could send a girl,
or a friend of mine over who was French. She replied, that I would have no
difficulties in getting her over. She said anyone would do it. There are
no difficulties involved.
In the hotel I again met Bernstein. He said that his son was coming
over from Concordia and he could take my baggage with him. I told him that I
was not ready yet, as my women had not arrived. He asked when I expected
them and I replied with the next train.
I saw the 4.39 p.m. boat off for Concordia. There were no suspicio[us]
looking women on board.
In the evening at 6p'm. I went with Berenstein to meet the train
also from Montevideo. There we met Yukelson. There were no foreign women on
this train, and we returned to the city. In the evening Yukelson, Berenstein
and I made a tour of the various cafes and cabarets.

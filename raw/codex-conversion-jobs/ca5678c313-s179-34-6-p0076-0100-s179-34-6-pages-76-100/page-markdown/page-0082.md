After taking some photographs I return/to Salte [handwritten: Salto written above 'Salte']
On my arrival at the hotel I found that Yukelson had been looking for me.
I went to his home and there found four immigrants, all Russians. Two
I had seen before. Yukelson explained that the other two had no money
and walked all the way from Montevideo to Salte, that it took them 20
days to make this journey and now they want to go accross but have no
money to pay him. The two men informed that they were deserters from the
Polish Army and arrived in Montevideo without papers about a month ago.
They could get no work and decided to smuggle accross into Argentine
without papers. They had relatives in Buenos Aires , they said. I
inquired where they learned that one can cross into Argentine from
Salte and was informed by them that they were told so by people in
Montevideo.

Before leaving Salte I stopped over to see Meise Yair.
He suggested that I see the manager of the Bank of London Salte Branch
saying that he might help me cross by telephoning to someone of the
English Meat packing plant on the Argentine side.

I left Salte on Saturday at about 5,30 p.m. Samuel Yukelson
saw me off. He told me that if my people are coming to telegraph him and
he would meet them at the train, take them home and see them safely
accross the boarder.

Respectfully submitted
P. M. Auerbach

```
                                                                                                21

(Uruguay, )...                                                  EXHIBIT I

                                                        LEAGUE OF NATIONS
                                                TRAFFIC IN WOMEN AND CHILDREN

                                                        February 16th, 1921

                                                Replies to Questionnaire I

I.          (a) Yes, with certain exceptions as to mere admission to a house of
            prostitution of inscribed women over 18 at time of passage of the act (October
            11th, 1916) and of married women. It is presumed that this latter exception
            permits the admission of married girls over 16 (the marriage age).
            (b) Yes.
            In case (a) the age of consent is 21 with the exceptions noted above.
            The legislation covering these offences is contained in the law above
            referred to and in certain regulations particularly that of October 29, 1920
            (copies of which are in my possession). Such acts are punishable if some part
            of the offence has been committed abroad.
            Penalties range from three months to 4 years depending not entirely
            on the age of the victim but other aggravating or extenuating circumstances.
            Statistics of prosecutions and convictions of international offences
            of this character have been requested from and promised by the Police Department.

II.         I am informed by the Police Department that the law above referred
            to is the only existing law on this subject. In addition there are the
            regulations authorized by law which I have.

III.        I am advised by the Police Department that an amendment of section
            4 of the above act is under consideration by which it is hoped to make it
            easier to convict men who live on the earnings of prostitutes.

IV.         Yes, by police vigilance against known caftens (Police Department
            Information).
```

EXHIBIT A

SOCIETE DES NATIONS
LEAGUE OF NATIONS
Montevideo, Uruguay,
June 20, 1924

QUESTIONS CONCERNING THE INTERNATIONAL TRAFFIC IN WOMEN
AND CHILDREN UNDER INVESTIGATION BY THE SPECIAL COMMISSION
OF THE LEAGUE OF NATIONS IN COLLABORATION WITH GOVERNMENTS.

1. Is there such a traffic for purposes of prostitution from other
   countries to Uruguay?
2. Is there such a traffic from Uruguay to other countries?
3. If the answer to either of the above questions is negative, what
   are the measures taken by the Uruguayan Government to
   prevent it?
4. If the answer to either question 1 or 2 is positive,
   (a) what is the extent of the traffic?
   (b) under what circumstances is it carried on?
5. Has the system of licensed houses of prostitution now in opera-
   tion in Montevideo increased or decreased the extent of
   this international traffic to or from Uruguay?
6. If the system of licensed houses is believed to have decreased
   this international traffic, can this decrease be shown by
   statistics?
7. Have you a list of persons suspected of importing to or exporting
   from Uruguay women or children for prostitution?
8. If so, can you furnish names and photographs of such traffickers
   for the use of other governments?
9. Can you furnish statistics of arrests and convictions of traffick-
   ers in women and children?
10. Can you give the names, nationalities, and dates of arrival in
    Uruguay of all foreign-registered prostitutes now active?

(Signed) BASCOM JOHNSON
Director of Field Investigations.

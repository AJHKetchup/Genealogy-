EXHIBIT J

ANNUAL REPORT OF GOVERNMENTS 1922
TRAFFIC IN WOMEN AND CHILDREN

1. No Central Authority has been appointed.

2. No Legislative Measures dealing directly with the traffic or in-
directly with matters arising therefrom during the year 1922. (There were
however certain executive decrees and regulations concerning "Pensiones de
artitas," Sanitary Inspection of Prostitution, and the Prostitution Medical
Service - see pages 272-280 of the Boletin de la Jefatura de Policia - Ano
IV - Num. III.

3. This information has been requested several times from the Police
Department and particularly by letter of July 1st, 1924 and definitely
promised by Mr. Carlos Fernandez - copy of this letter was handed Mr.
Fernandez on July 1st and official copy given the office of the Minister
of Foreign Affairs for official transmission on July 3rd, 1924.

4. Have no definite information on this subject but judge from
information furnished by Mr. Fernandez that this procedure is seldom used.

5. This information, in so far as it covers exclusions at the time
of entry, has been requested but not yet received. I do not understand
that the law or regulations cover deportation of alien prostitutes after
entry. The Police Department Secretary intimated very plainly that alien
prostitutes were regarded as a safeguard of the virtue of native women and
therefore welcome.

6. The Police Department state that their agents watch the railroad
and steamship terminals for caftens and girls or women traveling alone - that
the former are followed to their places of residence and watched for violations

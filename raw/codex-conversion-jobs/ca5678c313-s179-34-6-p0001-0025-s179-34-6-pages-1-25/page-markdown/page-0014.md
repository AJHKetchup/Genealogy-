There are also other Resolutions of the Police Service, such

4. The trade exists, but its extension is limited, as it
is only a derivative of that directed to the Argentine Republic.
The circumstances are clandestine, depending almost wholly on the
vigilance of the authorities in discovering such cases, and on ac-
count of the difficulty of obtaining confessions of the victims.
The chief location of the traffic is Buenos Aires, whence it expands
in all directions, one of which is Uruguay. The short distance
between Montevideo and Buenos Aires facilitates the plans and hid-
ing of the caftens and the dissimulation (camouflaging) of their
business, making more difficult preventive action by the Police.

5. Although there is a certain relation (affinity) between
houses of prostitution and the white slave trade, there does not
exist any increase or decrease as a consequence of the permitted
system. The basis of the decrease observed is the approval of the
Law of 1916 and the severity of the Police in its application, as
there are very few women that now come from abroad to this country
to practice prostitution. The system now permitted is at present
under observation. We look for better legislation.

6. The answer is deduced from the previous question. There
is no statistical information.

7. As we carry registers of prostitutes and of delinquen-
cy, we have a list of persons suspected of that trade (importing and
exporting women), over which corresponding vigilance is exercised.

8, 9 & 10. The same answer applies to these questions,
except the specification )personal data?) of women brought to the
country by tradesmen, and dates of their arrival, as the majority
of them have resided here for many years, and their civil identity
is generally hidden and changed by reason of mutual understanding.

## Literal Transcription
(Uruguay) - 3 -
Copies of unanswered questionnaires were furnished Dr. Paul-
ine Luisi who arrived in Montevideo a day or two before the departure
of the investigator. Some of the original telegrams tending to show
active participation by organizations and individuals in Montevideo
and Salto in clandestine immigration between Uruguay and Argentine,
were also given to Dr. Luisi for the purpose of discussion with the
Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the President of Uruguay, in the hope
that joint action by these two Governments might stop this practice.
Conference was had with Drs. Luisi and Otero at which the
action of the Uruguayan Government on various requests for information
from the Secretariat of the League of Nations was discussed. At this
conference it was learned that at least five of these questionnaires
had not been answered by the Government, whereupon the investigator
volunteered to attempt to prepare data which would enable the Govern-
ment to answer them with the understanding that some of the questions
could not be answered until material requested from the various De-
partments, and not yet furnished, had come to hand. In compliance
with this understanding, the investigator prepared on the boat be-
tween Montevideo and Santos, answers to the following questionnaires
which are attached hereto:

    Questionnaire I, League of Nations, Feb. 16, 1921, Exhibit I.
    Annual Report for 1922, Exhibit J.
    " " " 1923, Exhibit K.
    Letter of the Secretary-General of the League of Nations,

        traffic of the system of licensed houses. C.L.57(c).

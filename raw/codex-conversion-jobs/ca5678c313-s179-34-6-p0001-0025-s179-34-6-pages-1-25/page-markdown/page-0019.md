## Literal Transcription
(Uruguay, S. A.)
EXHIBIT E.
Montevideo, July 3d, 1924

His Excellency, The Minister of Foreign Affairs,
Montevideo, Oriental Republic of Uruguay.

Sir:

On leaving Uruguay, as I do July 4th, I wish to express my
deep appreciation of the real cooperation and unfailing courtesy which
I have received at your hands and also from all of your staff with
whom I have come into contact,- Dr. Moro Otero particularly.

In the course of my investigations I have had occasion to
submit several lists of questions to the Police Department and the
answers to the first list have already been filed in your office. I
am enclosing a second list which I should be glad to have translated
and submitted to any of the Departments which you think would have any
part of the information requested.

I should greatly appreciate your having the answers to these
questions collected and forwarded to me at any of the addresses on my
itinerary which I have given to Dr. Moro Otero.

As soon as I receive these answers and have had time to read
all the documents which I have collected, I shall prepare a report, a
copy of which will doubtless be transmitted to you by the Commission
which I represent.

With great respect, I am yours to command,

(Signed) BASCOM JOHNSON
Representing the Special Commission
to Investigate the International
Traffic in Women and Children in
Collaboration with Governments.

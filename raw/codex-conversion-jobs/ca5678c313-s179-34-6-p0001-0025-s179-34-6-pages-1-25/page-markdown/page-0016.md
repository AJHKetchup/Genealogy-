II. Does the Uruguayan Government notify the Argentine or
Brazilian Governments when prostitutes or caftens, either alone or to-
gether, leave Uruguay for such foreign countries?

III. Would you be kind enough to have prepared for me stat-
istics for each year since 1916 (the date of the passage of the Brum
Act) showing, first, the number of exclusions from the country of caf-
tens or their victims at the time of entry; second, the number of ar-
rests of such caftens; and, third, the number of convictions of caftens
for international traffic?

IV. May I remind you of your promise to furnish me with the
names and photographs of persons known to be or suspected of being
international caftens? In this connection I should particularly be
glad to have the photographs, finger prints and any other material you
have concerning the notorious caften known as "Le Charlotte". This ma-
terial would be of great value to the emigration countries of Europe
and I would greatly appreciate receiving it before I leave Montevideo
on July 4th, or, if that is not possible, having it reach me in New
York before September 1st, when I plan to return to Europe. My New York
address is 370 Seventh Avenue.

V. I understand you to reply to the 5th and 6th of my first
list of questions, first, that there is no relation, in your opinion,
between the system of licensed houses existing in Montevideo and the
international traffic in women; second, that a decrease in this traf-
fic in foreign women to Uruguay for prostitution has been noted due to
the rigorous enforcement by the police of the law of 1916 (Brum Act);
third, that the number of such foreign women who now come to Uruguay to
practice prostitution is very few. If I am correct in my understand-
ing of your answers, may I now ask, first, what, in your opinion, would
be the effect on such international traffic of a Government law or de-
cree forbidding the inscription and employment of foreign women in li-
censed houses of prostitution? Second, what are the statistics and
other facts on which you base your statement that the enforcement of
the Brum Act has brought about a decrease in international traffic?
Third, what is the explanation of the fact that the proportion of for-
eign women among the total of new inscriptions of prostitutes has in-
creased each year (for the last 5 years) beginning in 1919, at 25.7
per cent. and reaching in 1923 42.2 per cent?

Governments, lists, photographs, or finger prints of persons known to
be or suspected of being international caftens?

notifications that any such persons or their victims are believed to

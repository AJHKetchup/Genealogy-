July 2, 1924

S U M M A R Y . . . . . . .

Beginning June 15, 1924, an investigation of the Traffic
in Women and Children was undertaken in Montevideo, Uruguay. Up to
the present date approximately 147 houses in which prostitution is
practiced, were visited.

In these 147 houses I spoke to approximately 250 foreign
women. In this group is also included the madames of the houses. All
the women admitted to me that they had practiced prostitution prior
to coming to Montevideo, and fully 50 mentioned that they had had
their transportation paid by local disorderly-house keepers. Those
women also admitted that the advanced money had to be "worked off",
in the houses by the girls.

Among the 250 foreign women, at least 150 are French girls.
The madames in 10 houses were willing to accept unregistered girls.

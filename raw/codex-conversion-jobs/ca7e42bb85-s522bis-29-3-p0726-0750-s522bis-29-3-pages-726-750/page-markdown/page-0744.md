Oserai considerations The situation in Honduras is, from the economic point of view, exceedingly difficult. There have been days when, owing to lack of pay, the police have been on the point of striking. The lack of cooperation of tbs Government with the League is due largely to the fact that the whole attention of the Foreign Office is concentrated on Washington, where the Chief Justice -as arbitrator in the dispute -will give his sentence in regard to the cue stion of the frontier lines between Guatemala. The critical situation of the Government has obliged the Ministery to dismiss a large part of the staff and the employees who remain are not sufficient to cope with the work. I have seen there a quantity of European correspondence, including that of the League, which has not even been opened. On the other hand, the Government wishes to avoid all 'flirtation* with Europe until Washington has settled the que stion of the frontier lines.

In 1931 there were two revolutions, and it is very probable that before the next presidential elections, in October, another revolution of more serious proportions will break out.

In view of the above situation, it is not very surprising that Honduras has not sent any delegates to Geneva.

A little while ago I sent to the Information Section various communications with different publications, aiming them the Memorandum of the Government, Justice and Health, which, I believe, contain information of interest to the League, among the circulars which I received from the League there were some referring to the White Slave Traffic. In Honduras the White Slave Traffic is a thing

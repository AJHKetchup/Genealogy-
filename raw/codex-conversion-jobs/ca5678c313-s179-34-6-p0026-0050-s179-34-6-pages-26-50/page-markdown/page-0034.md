ty should be abolished is Clause 5, Art. 285 of the Civil Code.

        Art. 3. Art. 300 of the Penal Code is hereby modified,
substituting the expression "or corruption of minors" of Clause 1,
for "or proxenetism or other similar delinquencies".

        Art. 4: The executive power will take the necessary steps
to prevent the entrance into the Republic of all individuals known to
have engaged, in or out of the country, in the trade of women, until
the date of their departure for same. Notwithstanding this, should
anyone insist on landing, he can do so under the vigilance of the au-
thorities, and should make his claim within 24 hours before the corr-
esponding judge handling proxenetism matters. (1). Should he not make
his claim within 24 hours, he can be compelled to leave the country.
The judge will decide upon his claim, maintaining or annulling the
administrative measure, in audience with the police authorities and
the interested party, shortly and summarily, in accordance with his
conscience, being able to consider the information given by the said
authorities as sufficient proof. There will be no ulterior recourse
to the decision of the judge. Whenever it should be necessary in the
opinion of the Judge, he can exact ample bail to complement the proof
produced.

        Art. 5: The managers of prostitution houses, "casas de
oitas", actresses' boarding-houses with character of such, and any
other similar house should have in visible places copies of this law
and its regulating decree, in several languages, under penalty of
$100 to $200 fine, or equivalent imprisonment.

        Art. 6: Should any public employee counteract in any form,
either by acts or omissions the purpose of this law, if the guilt is
not wore he will be punished with the loss of his employment and in-
hability of from 2 to 4 years to occupy another post.

        Art. 7: In spite of the fact that the Executive Power will
make of the present law a regulation of general character, the same
will dictate as soon as possible a special regulation for the so-
called "Actresses" boarding-houses".

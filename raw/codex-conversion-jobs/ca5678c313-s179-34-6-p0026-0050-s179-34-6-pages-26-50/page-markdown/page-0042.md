The system of licensing renders the keeper of a brothel
almost a public servant and gives him the benefit of a seeming
moral guarantee and of a no less illusory guarantee of
hygienic conditions at the expense of the freedom of the
prostitutes - all this to enable him by trafficking in women,

men. To uphold this organised sexual traffic, which
originated in prejudice, was tolerated by the law and is
kept in existence by both, is to deny the social purpose of law
itself, since the form of vice which the law combats is
stimulated by the organisation it supports. There is no
valid reason for retaining this iniquitous institution. X It
is not necessary for the medical regulation of prostitution,
nor are the activities of the police, beyond their normal
duties of preventing offences against morality and decency in
public places, successful in their avowed object of organising
prostitution by restricting it to licensed houses by means of
a system of recruiting women which is not only sometimes unjust,
but in many cases iniquitous. Incontrovertible proof of
this is given by the following table, which should be
compared with the figures of the male population of the capital;
it sufficiently demonstrates that the system of tolerance,
which is the form the procurers have given to their infamous
traffic, bears no relation to the so-called useful purposes for
which it is maintained in a civilisation which ought long
since to have repudiated it.

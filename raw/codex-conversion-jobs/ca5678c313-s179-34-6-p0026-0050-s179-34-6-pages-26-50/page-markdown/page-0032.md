                                                EXHIBIT N

                        Repression of Proxenetism and Other Similar Delinquencies

                                                                                                Code).

                                                    Law

Legislative Power:

                        The Senate and House of Representatives of the Republica
Oriental del Uruguay, gathered in General Assembly

                                                    Resolve:

Art. 1: The Fourth Section, Title 8, Book No. 2 of the Penal
Code, will read as follows:

                        "Of the Proxenetism and Other Similar Delinquencies"

297. The following will be considered guilty of proxenetism:
        1. A person who facilitates directly or indirectly
        That who should obtain or contribute to the obtention by any
means other than the simple admission into a "tolerancia" house by the
manager of same, that a woman should maintain illicit relations with
one or more persons or abandon this country with that end in view,
even with the consent of the victim, or should retain her against
her will in a prostibule or similar house, even if apparently doing
lawful trade, under any pretext whatsoever.

        2. The manager of a "tolerancia" house who should knowingly
admit the victim of the guilt above mentioned, and in any case under-
age women, excepting those who, having attained the age of 18 should
be inscribed in the prostitution registers at the time of the sanc-
tion of this law.

        3. The person under whose guard, whether for tutorship or in-
struction, a minor not included in the exception mentioned in the
preceding paragraph should be, and that knowing of the habitual
prostitution of the minor should not protect her and try to prevent
its continuation, or does not place her at the disposal of the au-
thorities, if unable to safe-keep her owing to lack of means.

        4. Any individual who should exploit a prostitute and receive
money obtained through her trade, whether he lives with the woman
or not and has or not other means of livelihood.

        In the cases of Clause 1 the penalty will be of 2 to 4 years

(Uruguay.)                                          EXHIBIT M

                                            Questionnaire C.L. 61, 1924
                                            Commission of Experts
                                            Traffic in Women and Children

        1. Yes - the Government, particularly the Police Department and the
National Council of Hygiene, has such information and by a careful analysis
of records can furnish valuable data. No replies have been made to the
Advisory Committee. The Police Department claims to have a list of 1400
caftens and that this list contains dossiers of many of these persons in-
cluding photographs, nationalities, sex, et cetera. The method used by
these caftens (men generally) are substantially as follows:- To go or send
to Europe (often France) for professional prostitutes, pay their way to
South America, often after paying the caften or manager of the house where
they were employed a sum approximating $400.00 (American dollars) for their
release. If documents such as a passport and certificate of good moral
character cannot be obtained the men and girl get off at Rio and come to
Montevideo by train across the Uruguayan border at Rivera, thereupon the
woman is inscribed and enters a house of prostitution where she pays the
cost of her passage before she is allowed to keep any part of her earnings.
If her ultimate destination is Buenos Aires, she secures a cedula of identity
and a certificate of two years residence in Montevideo and goes to Buenos
Aires on the night boat. If this is difficult she takes the train for Salto
and, easily evading the Uruguayan and Argentine authorities crosses
clandestinely the Uruguayan River into Argentine at Concordia and thence
by train to Buenos Aires. It is rare that a good girl, or a girl under
21 years is thus brought. Professional prostitutes already trained are
regarded as better money makers than amateurs. Most of the women admit
to having practiced prostitution before leaving Europe.

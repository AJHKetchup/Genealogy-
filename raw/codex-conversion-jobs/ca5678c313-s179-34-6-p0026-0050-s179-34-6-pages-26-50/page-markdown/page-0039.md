Such an enactment would hardly confer excessive
powers on the Government. Aliens falsely accused of
procuring would enjoy legal guarantees which are greater
in the Bill which I have the honour to submit to your
consideration than under the legislation now in force,
since this Bill will oblige the authorities to go through
certain formalities in regard to expulsion which are now
left to the discretion of the executive, and the absence
or omission of which is liable to give rise to injustice.

The necessity of the second amendment is shown
by judicial experience. In dealing with inveterate
criminals - those whom it is most important to punish -
it is practically impossible to obtain a conviction for
procuring, owing to the difficulty of establishing the
full proof required by the Code of Criminal Procedure.
It is a notorious fact that the plea of "Not Guilty" on
which the delinquent founds his case is usually accom-
panied by the withdrawal of the denunciation or declara-
tion of the victim, under the brutal influence of the
procurer, who domineers over her and ill-treats her,
while the women who share her unhappy fate in a licensed
house, house of assignation or artistes' lodging-house -
all of which are nothing more nor less than brothels
carried on by the procurer - invariably refuse to speak
or feign ignorance.

In such cases the defence of society, to be
effective, must be based on the extension to cases of
procuring of the rules of presumptive evidence governing
appeals against summonses issued by the administrative
authorities. 2.

## Literal Transcription
The summons shall be given in the presence of
two responsible witnesses and shall state the day and hour
of its issue. Nevertheless, should any such person insist
on landing or remaining in the Republic, he may do so, but
subject to the supervision of the authorities, and within
48 hours of receiving the summons he must appeal to the
local magistrate competent to try cases of procuring. The
appeal may be made in writing on unstamped paper, or the
appellant may apply in person to the magistrate to record the
appeal officially. The clerk of the court shall report
immediately to the Department of the Chief of Police that the
appeal has been lodged, with a view to interrupting the period
of grace specified in the summons. If appeal is not made with-
in 48 hours, the person concerned shall be required to leave
the country. This provision cannot be enforced until the
Department of the Chief of Police has obtained a certificate
from the Clerk of the Court showing that at the time of the
issue of the certificate no appeal had been lodged against the
summons. The magistrate shall pronounce upon the appeal,
either upholding or reversing the administrative measure, and
the evidence of the police authorities and the accused shall
be heard. The magistrate's decision shall be given briefly
and summarily in accordance with the dictates of his conscience,
and he may be entitled to accept the information of the police
as sufficient proof. There shall be no appeal against the
magistrate's decision. The magistrate may, if he thinks fit,
require further sureties in support of the evidence.

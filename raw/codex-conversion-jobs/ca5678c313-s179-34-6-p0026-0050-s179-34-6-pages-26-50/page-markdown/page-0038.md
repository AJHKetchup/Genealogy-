```
                                                                                              36

of procurers known to the police entering the country
at points on the frontier which are not watched, and thus
escaping all molestation on the part of the police and
magistrates; the second is the fact that information
(furnished in accordance with the Convention of 1910)
concerning the delinquents is often not received from
abroad until they have landed. In both cases justice
is cheated, as Article 4 of the Law of October 20th 1916
is not applicable, and as the rules of presumptive evidence
which this article lays down in respect of appeals against
summons issued by the administrative authorities are
also inapplicable to cases of procuring.

It is therefore essential that two amendments
should be made in the existing legislation regarding alien
procurers. Firstly, the law should authorise the
expulsion of alien procurers settled in the country, or
temporarily resident therein, who have escaped the
vigilance of the immigration authorities and who are
acting as agents of the international associations
or carrying on their nefarious traffic on their own
account under cover of lawful occupations. These
persons often abuse the rights of citizenship by entering
their names on the civil registers in order to be
exempt from the measures applicable to undesirable aliens.
```

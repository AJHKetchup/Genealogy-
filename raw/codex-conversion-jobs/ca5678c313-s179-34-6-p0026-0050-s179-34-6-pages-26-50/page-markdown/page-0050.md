```
Proof of the existence of supplementary contracts infringing this pro-
hibition shall be deemed to be proof of the offence of procuring, and
the manager and the impresario of the theatre shall be held responsible
for that offence.

                                Article 10.

        Employment agencies shall be required to notify the police
authorities, under pain of being closed down - unless the omission
helps to convict them of the crime of procuring - of the destination
of women and children for whom they find employment. This destination
shall be ascertained by the police.

                                Article 11.

        Every immigrant, being a woman or girl under age and not being
accompanied by her parents, guardians or persons legally responsible
for her, shall notify the immigration officials of her destination
in the country, and shall be subject to supervision until the police
authorities inform her of the result of the enquiries that have been
made in this connection. In the case of minors, if the information
is unfavourable, the authorities will order them to leave the country,
if the person in charge if them does not undertake to change their
destination.

        The magistrate competent to try cases of procuring and to hear
the appeals mentioned in Article 4 of this Law are the judge of the
Correctional Court, and the Divisional Judges when no official of that
court is sitting.

                                Article 13.

        The laws of June 30th 1918 on the reduction of terms of imprison-
ment and of January 25th 1916 and June 8th 1921 on the conditional
suspension of sentences shall not be applicable to the cases covered
by the present law.
```

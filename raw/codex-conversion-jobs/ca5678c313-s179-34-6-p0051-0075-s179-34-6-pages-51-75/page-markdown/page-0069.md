```
                                                              Montevideo, June 28, 1924.

T. W. & C.
Salto, Uruguay and Concordia, Argentine.

                                I left Montevideo on Tuesday evening, June 24th and
arrived in Salto the following day at about 12.30 p.m. On my arrival
I was accosted by a police officer who asked my name and address which
I wrote for him on a piece of paper. The other passengers on the
train where asked the same questions. I went to the Concordia
Hotel and was met by a porter outside the hotel who later on gave his
name as Enriquez Menon and who said that he was Italian by birth.
I gave to this man on arrival in the room assigned to me the card that
P.K. had received from the Expresso International and asked him whether
he knew Gustav Rice of the Alhambra Hotel to which he replied that he
did not. I told him that I had no documents to cross into Argentine
and that Mr. Rice suggested that I go to Salto and that he would help
me to go to Concordia. He replied that he cannot remember Mr. Rice
but that I would have no difficulties in crossing into Argentine. I
inquired of him what was necessary and he said that I would need a
vaccination certificate and that was all that was asked of one crossing
the river. I asked him other questions but the bell rang and he had to
get out. When I left the room I found the card I had brought and given
this porter under the table in the corridor.

                                I went downstairs and then to the landing of the boats to
Concordia and inquired for the boat schedule which was given me as
follows : Leave Salto; 8A.M., 9 A.M., 10.30 A.M., 11.30 A.M.
1.30 P.M., 2.30 P.M., 4.30 P.M. and at sunset. Leave Concordia: 7.30 a.m
8.30 A.M., 10,a.m., 1 p.m., 3. P.M., 4 P.M. and sunset.
                                I saw the 1.30 P.M. boat leave for Concordia. The express
These launches are small sized affairs and have the capacity of carrying
about 20 passengers. There were 11 passengers on this boat, 3 women
```

Moises induced some of his friends to double their money in a easy way
and they put up several thousand Pesos. Sometime ago Yukelson introduced
to Moises a man who said that he had a machine which could make from
Pesos [handwritten: Pesos] two pesos by having it go through a certain process. Moises and his
friends put up the money, Yukelson did not [handwritten: but] expected a profit or a
percentage. Shortly after the money was turned over, the man disappeared
with all the money entrusted to him. This was given me little by little
by Moises. He also told me that he tried to shot Yukelson and he showed
me a hole in the wall made by a bullet. He said that he had engaged a man
to kill Yukelson. Berenstein tried to act as mediator and said that he
would take it up with Yokelson to settle the matter, as it was well
understood such matters could not be taken up by the courts.

Moises told me, that he would see that I and my friends would be
taken over to the Argentine side for 5 Pesos each. I inquired whether
it would not be possible to bribe our way through the officials, and he
said that this was not possible as he had tried it in other instances and
that he had failed. He said, that at any time I [handwritten: me] was ready to come back
to him and he would take care of him. I reminded him that [handwritten: my] companions
were women and not used to hardship and he said that there [handwritten: was] no hard-
ship attached to it. He asked whether we had any baggage and I said that
we had several pieces. He informed me that it would be necessary to get
the baggage over in a regular way. He was about to explain how when a

In the evening Berenstein and I [handwritten: to] went a disorderly house in

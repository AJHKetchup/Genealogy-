The SECRETARY-GENERAL.

Baron Drachenfels, Secretary-General
of the 13th International Red Cross Conference,
which will begin at the Hague on October 23rd
next, telephoned to me yesterday and asked
whether any member of the Disarmament Section
would be present at that Conference. I said
that I thought that the Secretary-General had,
as in previous years, decided that Dame Rachel
Crowdy should represent him at the Conference.
Baron Drachenfels said that he was aware of that,
but drew my attention to the fact that the agenda
of the Conference contained a number of questions
which were certainly in no way social questions.
He thought that these questions were much more
closely connected with the work of the Disarma-
ment Section.
I said that I did not really remember
exactly how the matter stood, and would not fail
to look up the file, but I could not promise
any further action on the part of the Secretary-
General in this matter.

settled by you. In your letter of the 23rd

would be represented by Dame Rachel Crowdy, and

that you saw no reason to alter that statement.

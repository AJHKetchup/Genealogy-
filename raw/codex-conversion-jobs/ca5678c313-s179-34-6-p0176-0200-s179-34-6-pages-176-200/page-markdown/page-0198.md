July 8, 1924

<u>REGISTRATION OF PROSTITUTES.</u>

In the Police Department and the Department of Health where I called for information regarding the registration of prostitutes and the system used by them, I was given the following:

In order to become a registered prostitute, a woman must first apply to the Police Department, Division of Identification, and secure a cedula de identificacion (Certificate of Identification). In order to get this certificate, she is asked to produce a birth certificate, a passport or other document, that would prove her identity; orobring witnesses to show that what she is telling is the truth.

In order to secure a cedula, she is fingerprinted and photographed, and among the questions asked and which are filled in on the application form, herewith attached as Exhibit A, are her first and second name; daughter of?; born when? (day, month, year); age of her parents; her business; marital status; and if married, the name of her husband; place she was originally employed; residence; how she identifies herself and documents furnished therefor.

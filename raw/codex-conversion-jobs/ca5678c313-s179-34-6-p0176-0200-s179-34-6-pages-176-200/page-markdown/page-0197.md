(Montevideo, #2)

sold his interest in the Hotel Europa in Montevideo to one Abel
Bolicaux; as well as his various disorderly houses in Montevideo to
other people. The Chief promised to let me have a photograph and a
copy of Charlotte's record before I left Montevideo on July 4th, and
that this material would be sent to my steamer, but this promise ap-
parently was not kept, as I never received the promised picture, al-
though I received material on some other subject,--prostitution.
     Later Wolf,( the police official present when I had my con-
versation with his Chief, and who was assigned by the Chief of Police
to act as Major Johnson's interpreter,)informed me that it seemed
strange to him that Charlotte's picture should have disappeared from
the Book of Notorious Kaftans and that his name should be rubbed off
the same. He said that he noticed by looking through this Book of
Kaftans' Photographs that at the place where Charlotte's picture had
formerly been, the writing under the missing photo had been erased.
I asked Wolf how this could be possible and was told by him that
Charlotte was a very influential man in this community on account of
his wealth; that he had police officials on his payroll; and whenever
certain police officials needed any money, Charlotte would supply it.
He also said that if the police really wanted to arrest Charlotte, they
could have done it very easily;a couple of months ago, Wolf said, he
saw Charlotte in Montevideo, and he spoke to him. When I asked Wolf
why he had not arrested Charlotte then, knowing that he is a fugitive
from justice, with a sentence of 15 months' imprisonment facing him,
Wolf said that he had no orders to arrest Charlotte and did not want
to get himself in trouble with higher officials in his department.

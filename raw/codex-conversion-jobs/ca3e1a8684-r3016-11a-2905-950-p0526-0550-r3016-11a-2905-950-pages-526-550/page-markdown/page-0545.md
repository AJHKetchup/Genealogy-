In connection with chemical warfare, the progress of technology is in adverse ratio to considerations of human sympathy; and the German Red Cross sees only one means of rendering impossible chemical attacks upon civilian populations, namely, by a strict prohibition of bombing aircraft. The evil will be struck at its root, if, not only the application of chemical weapons and the training in these arms during peace be prohibited, but also the use of bombing aircraft and all preparatory measures for that purpose. Such a prohibition would not only remove a threatening danger from the civilian population but would, at the same time, delay the outbreak of wars, since it would do away with the most dangerous of all weapons of attack.

The German Red Cross desires to suggest that the XIII th. International Red Cross Conference should entrust the Expert Commission without prejudice to the other tasks of that Commission with an axamination of the above matter. With this in view, the German Red' Cross suggests the addition of further technicians and jurists to the said Commission. The technicians and jurists to be appointed would, in particular, have to examine the administrative and penal measures by which a prohibition of the above mentioned kind could be internationally enforced.

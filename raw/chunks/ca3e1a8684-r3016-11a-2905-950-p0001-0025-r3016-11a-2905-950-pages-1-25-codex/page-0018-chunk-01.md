THE SECRETARY-GENERAL.

        The Thirteenth Conference of the International
Red Cross Societies which I attended on your behalf
as representative of the Secretariat of the League of
Nations took place at the Hague and lasted from October
23rd to October 27th.
        Thus was a heavily laden Agenda, consisting of
eighteen items, some of them matters of wide importance;
but owing to the fact that the Conference divided its
work among four committees, that no time was wasted
and that the Conference was one of the best organised
that I have ever attended the work was done quickly
and well.

                                        Committee I. dealt with the following
subjects:

                1) the report of the International Committee
        of the Red Cross Societies on the funds which it
        administrates; administr's.

                2) the report of the International Committee
        of the Red Cross Societies on its activities during
        the period covered by the years 1925-1928;

                3) exemption from customs and transport duties
        on material sent to the International Institute
        for the Study of Medical Equipment and to the
        International Committee for the Standardisation of
        Medical Equipment;

                4) the use of the Red Cross as an aid to the
        victims of sea warfare;

                5) the Union International de Secours and the
        International Red Cross Societies;

                6) the proposal by Dr. Boland for the creation
        of an international Red Cross fleet;

                                                                                (OVER)

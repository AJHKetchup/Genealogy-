                                MINUTE SHEET

Dame Rachel CROWDY.

        Your note of yesterday, on the subject of your
going to the Hague to represent the Secretary-General at
the 13th International Red Cross Conference, crossed my
note to you on exactly the same subject this morning,
and I think that my note has already given a sufficient
reply to the question you raise.
        I _believe_ that it would be quite sufficient if the
Disarmament Section had a complete set of the documents
distributed to the Conference regarding the various items
on the agenda of interest to the Disarmament Section.
This documentation should naturally include minutes of
the discussions in so far as such minutes will be available.
        In your note you mention only Points IX and X of
the agenda. I have not the full agenda before me, but I
have some idea that perhaps one or two other items on the
agenda may be of interest to the Disarmament Section. I
think I marked these items in blue pencil on the copy of
the agenda contained in the registry file. The point is
of course not of great importance, as you will no doubt
acquire a full set of _all_ the documents of the Conference.
        I do not think it would be wise for the Disarma-
ment Section to make any comments on any of the items of
the agenda, or bring up any points to which the attention

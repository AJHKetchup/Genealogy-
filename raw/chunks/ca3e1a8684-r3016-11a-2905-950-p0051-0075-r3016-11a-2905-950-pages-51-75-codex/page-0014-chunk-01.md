## Literal Transcription

Memorandum.
Mr Smets and I discussed the attached papers
with the Secretary General this morning.

The Secretary General was of opinion that
Dr Kulz' request could only be refused if funds were
not available under the item in question since the
conversations which will be held at the Hague meeting
will have the greatest importance for the future work
and organisation of the International Relief Union.
It was consequently agreed - after I had waived my
previous objections of principle in view of the
important considerations brought forward by the Secretary
General - that Dr Külz expenses could be informed that
his expenses to the Hague would be reimbursed provided
there was a sufficient margin after providing for a
further meeting of the Organisation Committee before the
end of the year. As this most certainly seems to be the case,
Dr Kulz could be informed that the League will bear his
expenses.
Vu f. Richmond
16-X-28

lia / 2905 / 950

Dr. Brile

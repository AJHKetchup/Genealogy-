MONTEVIDEO, Uruguay, S. A.
June 22, 1924

IN RE POLICE ACTION.
---

On June 20th, 1924, Maj. Johnson and I accompanied by Dr.
Jose A. Mora Otero of the League of Nations Division of the Uruguayan
Ministry of Foreign Relations, called on Sr. Juan C. Gomez Folle, the
Chief of Police of Montevideo, but owing to the lateness of the hour
of our visit, and other difficulties, not much could be discussed.
We were told that an official interpreter of the Police Department
would call on us the same evening and arrange for an appointment for
the following day.

On the evening of the same day, a Mr. Wolf, interpreter
of the Police Department, called at our hotel and informed us that
next Saturday he would call and take us to Headquarters for a con-
ference with his Chief.

On Saturday he came back as promisedand a conference was
had with Sr. Paulo Ferrando, Secretary General of the Police Depart-
ment, who appeared to be an intelligent man and well versed on the
subject of prostutution and allied topics. Sr. Ferrando had with him
copies of the laws dealing with Prostitution, Traffic and its allied
topics, and said that he was making a compilation of them and would
give it to us before we departed. Maj. Johnson gave him a list of 10
questions which was prepared at the request of the Chief of Police,
and we were promised an answer by next Monday or Tuesday; he also
promised that after the questions are answered by him, he would submit
an additional memorandum; and also give us the names, photographs,

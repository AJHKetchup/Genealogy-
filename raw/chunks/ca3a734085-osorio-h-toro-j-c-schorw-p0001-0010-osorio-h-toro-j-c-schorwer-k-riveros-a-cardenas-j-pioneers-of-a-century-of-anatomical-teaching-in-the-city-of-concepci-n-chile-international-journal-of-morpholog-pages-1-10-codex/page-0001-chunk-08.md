The extensive anatomical activity of the University of
Concepción has been going on for 100 years since its beginnings. More
than a hundred professors have participated in it, training thousands of
professionals who contribute to the country. From this long list, five
pioneers who have stood out among their peers stand out. However,
the contribution of all its professors, assistants and workers is invaluable.

Currently, the teaching of anatomy is a reality in the eight
universities of the BioBio Region; of these, the San Sebastián, Andrés
Bello and Las Américas Universities have departmentalized the
anatomical activity.

Despite the passing of a century of anatomical activity, the
phrases of the famous doctor Dr. Lucas Sierra written in 1924 are still
valid: “.... It seems to us an unavoidable matter and of the greatest
importance that the professor of anatomy, convinced that in pure
science there is nothing that is not or can not be of great importance,
knows how to insist before his students, students of medicine only, on
the facts of real and positive use for the practical doctor; that he does
not fall into the temptation of making him learn even the most insignificant
roughness of a bone or the innumerable anomalies that I can offer in its
insertion this or that muscle; that is nothing more than “anatomical
pedantry” and does not contribute but to distance the taste for the study
of anatomy, which any doctor worthy of his name would do well to
preserve throughout his life..... let us then teach LIVING ANATOMY”
(Sierra, 1924).

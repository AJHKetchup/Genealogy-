                                                                11a / 2905 / 950
                                                                        950 III

                                MINUTE SHEET
                                Dame Rachel Crowdy.
                                THE SECRETARY-GENERAL.

                Baron Drachenfels, who for a long time
                has been Secretary to the Red Cross Societies
                in Geneva, called on me yesterday and handed
                me the annexed copy of the Programme for the
                XIIIth International Red Cross Conference,
                which is to take place in October 1928 at the
                Hague. He said that at previous Conferences
                the Secretary-General had nominated Dame Rachel
                Crowdy to represent him. Dame Rachel, however,
                had, unfortunately, not been able to come. For
                this reason, and in view of the importance of
                the next Conference, Baron Drachenfels - who
                I understand is to be its Secretary-General -
                expressed the hope that the Secretariat might
                perhaps take steps to be at all events repre-
                sented at the Conference. In this connection
                he pointed out that there are a number of
                important items on the provisional agenda of
                the Conference which fall entirely outside the
                field of action of the Social Section.

<u>SECRETARY-GENERAL:</u>

          There is a shadow of truth in what Baron
Drachenfels says, though hardly sufficient to
warrant his sweeping statement!
          Four Red Cross Conferences have been held
since the League of Nations came into
existence. I have been nominated (generally
with one other member) to represent the
Secretary-General at all four. (This was in
                                     is peculiar of
          ~~addition to~~ an Extraordinary Conference at
Berne to which I believe some member of the
Secretariat went as observer.)

          IX. I attended throughout. (1920).
          X. I attended throughout. (1921).
          XI. I attended all but the last day (1923)
when the Obscene Publications Conference, of
which I was Secretary, met.

          XII. Dr. Norman White and I were both
nominated (very much at the last moment) to this
Conference as representing the Secretary-
General. I am referred to in these words:
"J'ai également designé Dame Rachel Crowdy
pour le cas ou il lui serait possible d'être
de retour à Genève avant la fin de la Con-
férence."

          I was at the time attending another
Conference in London and as far as I remember
got back only in time for the last day. Dr.
Norman White presumably attended throughout.
"
          I have, as you know, a particular interest
in the International Red Cross, but if you feel
it wiser in view of Baron Drachenfels' state-
ment to appoint some other member of your staff
to attend the XIII Conference I shall quite
understand, although I should regret!

                                            Rachel F. Crowdy
                                            24. 5. 28.

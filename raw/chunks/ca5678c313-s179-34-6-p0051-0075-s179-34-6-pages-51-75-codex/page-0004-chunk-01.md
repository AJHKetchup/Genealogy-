To the Attorney-General (Senor Fiscal de lo Civil)

I have the honour to inform you that, for reasons
of which you are already aware, and in order to improve
the conditions of health, comfort, security and care
governing the accommodation of minors arrested on a
governmental warrant, it has been decided that henceforth
the police, when taking these persons in charge, will
place them in the Home of the Good Shepherd, where a
special section is being set aside for this purpose.
It has been decided that minors shall be conveyed
directly to the home from wherever they are arrested,
regardless of the hour of their arrest and without passing
through intermediate official channels, unless a contrary
order is given by the Government, in which case this
must be mentioned in the daily report.
The police agents effecting these arrests will
demand a receipt when handing over minors to the home,
together with mention of the time of day. Whenever
possible, they will allow minors to be accompanied
by members of their family or bonafide attendants and to
use any means of transport at their disposal which may
be better than these provided by the police official.

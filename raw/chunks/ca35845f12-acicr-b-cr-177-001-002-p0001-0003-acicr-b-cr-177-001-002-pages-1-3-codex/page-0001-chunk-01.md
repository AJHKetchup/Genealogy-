```text
CR 177-1

CR 177 DIPLOMATIC CONFERENCE 1.

1.-24.6.28. Let's transmit to Mr. Ratzenberger of the Federal Political Department in Bern 3 copies of the "Wishes and Resolutions voted at the XIIth Conference of 1925.

2.- 26.5.28. Let's send to Mr. Ratzenberger 3 copies of No 57 of the Review

3.- 30.6.28. Letter from Mr. Boissier to Mr. Huber regarding the functions of the Secretary General of the Conference, a position that Mr. Des Gouttes would occupy.

1 ann [1 attachment] 4.- 2.7.28. Letter from Mr. Motta, informing that the Diplomatic Conference will be convened for the second fortnight of June. Sends a circular note that the Department addresses to the States participating in the Geneva Conferences.

ms.+transcr. [manuscript + transcription] 5.- 3.7.28. Note from Mr. Huber concerning the representation of the IC [International Committee] at the Conference

6.- 6.7.28. Let's respond to the Political Department (4), thank them and we will collaborate on the improvement of the Convention.

7.- 7.7.28. Mr. Boissier to Mr. Huber, we transmit to him the letter above and the letter from the department of 2.7. (4)

8.- 7.7.28. Let's inform Mr. Dinichert that Mr. Des Gouttes has accepted to be Secretary General of the Conference. We will designate delegates if States accept that the IC be invited to participate in the Conference.

9.- 10.7.28. Mr. Dinichert thanks for the letter of 7.7. (8) and is pleased with the nomination of Mr. Des Gouttes. He does not think there is an obstacle to his function as Secretary General depriving him of his participation in discussions in commissions.

10.- 31.5.28. Mr. Motta recalls our letter of 14.6.26 extending the principles of the Geneva Convention to aerial warfare. Believes he must propose to the Federal Council to renounce adding the question of the immunization of medical aircraft to the program of the future Diplomatic Conference.

11.- 11.6.28. Let's respond to the letter above, We share the point of view.

12.- 1.10.28. Let's ask Mr. Lipiansky of the Hachette bookstore to obtain a separate printing of 500 copies of Mr. Werner's Course on POWs [Prisoners of War], published by this bookstore.

13.- 1.10.28. Let's send to Mr. Decencière in Paris the manuscript of Mr. Werner on POWs

14.- 8.10.28. Hachette bookstore responds to the letter of 1.10. (12), transmits the request to the Board of Directors of the Academy of International Law.

15.- 14.10.28. Hachette bookstore informs that the Academy of International Law authorizes the separate printings, but they must not be put on the market.

1 enveloppe [1 envelope] 16.- 17.10.28. Hachette bookstore informs Mr. Werner that it will have the separate printing of his course printed.
```

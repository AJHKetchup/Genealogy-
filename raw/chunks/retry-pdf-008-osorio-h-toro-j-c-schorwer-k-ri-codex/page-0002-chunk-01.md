Pioneers  of  a  Century  of  Anatomical  Teaching  in  the  City  of  Concepción,  Chile  
This  initiative  was  supported  by  the  tireless  work  of  a  committee  that  
advocated  the  joint  creation  of  three  vital  pillars:  a  University,  a  Clinical  
Hospital  and  a  Faculty  of  Medicine.  The  latter  was  to  be  closely  linked  
to  the  Hospital.  The  foundation  of  these  institutions,  not  without  
difficulties,  was  achieved  at  different  times,  the  first  of  which  was  the  
creation  of  the  University  of  Concepción  in  1919  (Muñoz,  1993).
The  desire  to  develop  university  education  in  the  city  of  
Concepción  began  in  the  18th  century.  In  this  city,  located  about  500  
kilometers  south  of  Santiago,  the  Jesuit  order  founded  the  Universidad  
Pencopolitana  under  the  auspices  of  the  Bishopric  of  Concepción.  This  
institution,  which  operated  between  1724  and  1767,  had  the  aspiration  
of  developing  a  School  of  Medicine,  which  was  cut  short  by  the  violent  
earthquake  and  tsunami  of  1751.  In  this  catastrophe,  the  city  had  to  
move  its  location  from  the  current  Penco  to  the  valley  of  Mocha,  located  
between  the  Biobio  and  Andalien  rivers.  Added  to  this  was  the  expulsion  
of  the  Jesuits  in  1767,  which  ultimately  postponed  the  creation  of  this  
School  (Wilhelm,  1962;  Cavieres,  2013).

OSORIO,   H.;   TORO,   JC;   SCHORWER,   K.;   RIVEROS,   A.   &amp;   CARDENAS,   J. Pioneers   of   a   century   of   anatomical   teaching   in   the   city   of   Concepción. Int.   J.   Morphol., 38(3):658-658,   2020.

Despite   his   travels   for   further   training,   his   connection   with   the University   of   Concepción   was   permanent,   where   he   taught   anatomy   for   the Schools   of   Medicine   and   Dentistry.   Between   1970   and   1974   he   was   Director of   the   Department   of   Anatomy.   His   extensive   academic   contribution   is reflected   in   more   than   50   cadaveric   preparations   that   are   part   of   the Anatomy   Museum   of   this   university.   In   addition   to   the   above,   there   are   20 anatomical   plates   painted   in   watercolor   and   multiple   published   investigations, several   of   them   with   his   teacher   Dr.   Solervicens.   In   1977   he   returned   to France   to   be   treated   for   an   illness   caused   by   exposure   to   formalin.   He retired   in   1996   and   died   in   2008   (Archives   of   the   Department   of   Normal Anatomy   and   Legal   Medicine,   University   of   Concepción).

The   extensive   anatomical   activity   of   the   University   of Concepción   has   been   going   on   for   100   years   since   its   beginnings.   More than   a   hundred   professors   have   participated   in   it,   training   thousands   of professionals   who   contribute   to   the   country.   From   this   long   list,   five pioneers   who   have   stood   out   among   their   peers   stand   out.   However, the   contribution   of   all   its   professors,   assistants   and   workers   is   invaluable.

Currently,   the   teaching   of   anatomy   is   a   reality   in   the   eight universities   of   the   BioBio   Region;   of   these,   the   San   Sebastián, Andrés Bello   and   Las   Américas Universities   have   departmentalized   the anatomical   activity.

Despite   the   passing   of   a   century   of   anatomical   activity,   the phrases   of   the   famous   doctor   Dr.   Lucas   Sierra   written   in   1924   are   still valid:   '…..   It   seems   to   us   an   unavoidable   matter   and   of   the   greatest importance   that   the   professor   of   anatomy,   convinced   that   in   pure science   there   is   nothing   that   is   not   or   can   not   be   of   great   importance, knows   how   to   insist   before   his   students,   students   of   medicine   only,   on the   facts   of   real   and   positive   use   for   the   practical   doctor;   that   he   does not   fall   into   the   temptation   of   making   him   learn   even   the   most   insignificant roughness   of   a   bone   or   the   innumerable   anomalies   that   I   can   offer   in   its insertion   this   or   that   muscle;   that   is   nothing   more   than   'anatomical pedantry'   and   does   not   contribute   but   to   distance   the   taste   for   the   study of   anatomy,   which   any   doctor   worthy   of   his   name   would   do   well   to preserve   throughout   his   life…..   let   us   then   teach   LIVING   ANATOMY' (Sierra,   1924).

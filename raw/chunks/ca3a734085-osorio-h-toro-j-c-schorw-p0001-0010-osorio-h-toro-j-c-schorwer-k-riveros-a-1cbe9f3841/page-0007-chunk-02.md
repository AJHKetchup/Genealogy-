He held numerous important positions at the University of Concepción. He was a professor
of anatomy and Director of the Department between 1961 and 1969. In 1969, during the
university reform, he was elected Rector, a position he held until 1972. In 1973, he was
appointed Minister of Education by President Salvador Allende Gossens, which led to his
being detained on Dawson Island in 1974 and subsequently sent into exile. He first spent
time in England and then settled in Mexico. In 1979 he became a professor of anatomy at
the Autonomous Metropolitan University of Xochimilco. In 1980 he was reinstated at the
School of Medicine of the University of Concepción, and in 1991 he was named Professor
Emeritus. In 1980 he published the text “Anatomy of the central nervous system” and
later wrote his memoirs called “In the name of a life”. At the age of 83, he died on
November 1, 1996. (Solervicens: Archives of the Department of Normal Anatomy and
Legal Medicine, University of Concepción).

**Anatomy Museum.** Morphological legacy. Scientific
Heritage of Chile, the Anatomy Museum of the University
of Concepción was founded in the 1930s by Dr.
Solervicens to help in the teaching, research and training
of health professionals. To do so, he had the valuable
collaboration of his disciple Mario Echeverría.
Today, the Anatomy Museum is visited annually by more
than 2,500 people, being an attraction in Penquista on
Cultural Heritage Day. Located in a large room inside the
anatomy building, this museum brings together in different
display cases the main organs, systems and topographic
regions that make up the human body. They address

cadaveric preparations in adults, children and fetuses.
Among its showcases are preparations with the repletion-
corrosion technique, which was applied in tracheobronchial
distribution, hepatic and coronary vascular distribution.
Other points of interest are the forensic medicine section
where injuries caused by direct trauma, ballistic trauma or
burns, whether by fire or electricity, are evidenced (Faculty
of Medicine, University of Concepción, 2019).

** Mario Echeverría is one of the greatest exponents of the heyday of
anatomy. He was born in Chillán on September 18, 1926, where he studied at the Liceo
de Hombres. In 1951 he graduated as a Dental Surgeon from the University of Concepción,
and in 1977 he also received his degree as a Medical Surgeon. In 1951 he began his
academic career at the Universidad Concepción as an assistant in the Operative Clinic.
The following year he joined the Department of Anatomy as a full-time professional
assistant. Between 1958 and 1959, as a result of a scholarship granted by the French
Government, he studied at the Université de Lyon where he worked with Michael Latarjet.
He also spent time in Amsterdam from 1965 to 1966 and in the anatomy laboratory of the
Faculté de Médecine at the Université de Paris V, with Professor André Delmás
(1966-1968 and 1978-1980). This institution appointed him Assistant Étranger and Attache-
Assistant de Sciencies Fondamentales; he also received certification as a specialist in
neurology from the Faculté de Médecine at the Université Pierre et Marie Curie in
conjunction with L’Hôpital Pitié-Salpêtrière. The fruits of his academic development in
France earned him the “Laureat De L’Academie Nationale” award in 1981.

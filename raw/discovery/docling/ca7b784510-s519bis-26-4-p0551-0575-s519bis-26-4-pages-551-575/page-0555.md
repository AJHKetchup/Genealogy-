Santo Domingo City,

April 13th 1932.

Mr. Comert, Director of* the Section of Information of the League of Nations, Geneva.

Dear Mr. Comert:

I have no letter from you, to which I might

refer.

Notwithstanding having sent you my letters by airway it appears that you receive them very late. I ignore the cause.

I enclose a copy of a letter that I wrote to the General Secretary lately and as a proof to what I say in said letter, I send you the cutting of the news¬ paper that refers to the ratification of the Statute of the Permanent Court of International Justice.

The Advising Commission of the Department of Foreign Relations, continues working in the affairs of the League of Nations. I am always in communication with the Secretary of this Commission, Mr. Calderdn, who is a good friend of mine, and, has as I, great interest for everything that concerns the League of Nations.

In these days I have given to some teachers here tjie pamphlet fcr the teaching of the ends and organi¬ sation of' the League. My program respecting this teaching is followed in the official and private Normal Schools of this country.

With my sincere regards for you I remain, Mr. Comert,

Yours respectfully,

(signed) ANA T. PARADAS.

Santo Domingo City, Dom. Rep.,

April 4th 1932.

To the Honourable Sir Erie Drummond, Secretary of the League of Nations, Geneva.

Honoured Sir:

Some days ago I sent you the documents which I had promised in my letter, dated 6th of March, in care of Mr.

Nogueira.

At present, the Advising Commission of the Department of Foreign Relations, is working in the affairs concerning the League of Nations; and according to what the Secretary of said Commission, Mr. Telésforo R. Calderon, have told me, they have begun to ratify the Statute of the Permanent Court of International Justice.

Your irrevocable resignation as Secretary of the League, has been very much felt here, and we hope that ypu will not deprive your valuable services to this important International Institution. I am sending you a cutting of your picture, which was published in one of our newspapers on occasion of your renunciation.

I am, Honourable Sir, Yours respectfully,

(signed) ANA. T. PARADAS.

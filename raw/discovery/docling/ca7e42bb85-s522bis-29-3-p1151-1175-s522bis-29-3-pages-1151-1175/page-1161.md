## LETTER FROM ARGENTINA

## NEED OF FINANCIAL COOPERATION BETWEEN THE UNITED STATES AND ARGENTINA

<!-- image -->

Commercial and financial relations between the United States and Argentina become every day more critical. Argentina on her part continues making efforts to maintain - and maintains punctually - the interest and amortization service of the external debt, paying in depreciated pesos. The difficulty in finding exchange and the unjustified low value of the peso have immobilized in Argentina more than 80,000,000 pesos in profits belonging to American industries: radio, movie films, phonograph records, automobiles, etc. The United States continues to be indifferent to this effort.

Argentina for some months has had a favorable balance of trade which up to May last amounted to 132,524,000 Argentine gold pesos; imports in this period reaching a total of 152,577,000 pesos and exports 285,101,000 pesos. Argentina has disposed of its entire wheat crop, while Canada and the United States, with artificial protective devices, still possess enormous surpluses.

Meanwhile, the rising tide of Argentine public opinion in favor of a moratorium of the foreign debt becomes more and more serious, and it is possible that our government may be obliged, in spite of its disinclination, to take into account this way of thinking, at least so far as relates to the amortization of these loans.

What reply will the united States of America give to this question, in so far as it refers to the Argentine Republic? Possibly the idea embodied in the

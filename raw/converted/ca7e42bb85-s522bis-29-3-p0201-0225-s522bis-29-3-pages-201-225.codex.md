TheThe Permanent Court of International Justice, in its Eighteenth (Ordinary) Session, delivered Advisory Opinion No. 18 concerning the Free City of Danzig and the International Labour Organization. The session was held on August 26th, 1930, with the file reference F. c. XX. and docket XVIII. 2.

The Court was composed of:
*   **President**: MM. Anzilotti
*   **Vice-President**: Huber
*   **Judges**: Loder, Nyholm, de Bustamante, Altamira, Oda, Fromageot, Sir Cecil Hurst
*   **Deputy-Judge**: M. Yovanovitch

The opinion addresses a resolution adopted by the Council of the League of Nations on May 15th, 1930. This resolution requested the Permanent Court of International Justice to provide an advisory opinion, in accordance with Article 14 of the Covenant, on the following question:

"Is the special legal status of the Free City of Danzig such as to enable the Free City to become a Member of the International Labour Organization?"

The Council further authorized its Secretary-General to submit this request to the Court, provide assistance in the examination of the question, and take necessary steps for representation before the Court.
